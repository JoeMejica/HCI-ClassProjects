Enhancing the Visitor Experience

The Georgia History Museum has proposed to allocate a large portion of the construction budget towards enhancing the visitor experience through technology. The following sections showcase three of the latest interaction technologies that are slated to be implemented in the museum.



Virtual Reality will Catch Your Eyes

With the consumer release of the Oculus Rift, the use of virtual technology at the Georgia History Museum will not only allow visitors to experience state-of-the-art technology but bring them closer to historical moments through 3D simulations and storytelling!

One of the proposed Virtual Reality exhibits proposed to the board of directors, is a VR simulation of key Civil War battles that took place in Georgia. This will allow visitors to simulate the experience of being on the battle field during a key era in American history.

As VR technology contines to improve, more VR exhibits are proposed to be implemented and featured!

Augmented Reality is Pretty Cool Too.

Many of the exhibits can utilize the use of augmented reality technology simply by developing around existing exhibit elements and structures. The Georgia History Museum hopes to obtain a businiess agreement with Microsoft to utilize the new Hololens for select exhibits! One of the proposed uses of the technology at the museum is allowing visitors to use the Hololens to dynamically intereact and see the changes of historical locations of Georgia over time including Atlanta! Additionally, we hope to allow users to access the web to research and learn more via the technology's web browser to futher enhance the educational aspect!

Additinally, GHM hopes to partner with the developers of Layar, a mobile augmented reality application to bring lower level augmented reality to visitors at the conveneience of their mobile devices. The goal is to implement the API within a dedicated Georgia History Museum app to use the augmented reality to access additional information about an exhibit and more!

Digital Signage will guide the way.

The use of digital signage technology has become more widespread use across univerisity campuses by displaying building maps, event information, social media feeds, and more. Joe Mejica, the lead developer of the Georgia History Museum website project has brought forth the proposal of utilzing Visix Digital Signage solutions to the museum as he also works as an AV Engineer specializing in digital signage at Georgia State University.

New digital signage technology has been developed to be more interactive with user by utilzing touch panel TV monitors to allow visitors to navigate to desired locations of the museum in addition to being able to browse different modules that digital signage has to offer including learning more about the current exhibit that the visitor is locatd in, browing social media feeds hosted by the museum, and more! Not only using digital signage will enhance the user experience at the information level but also bring a level of state-of-the-art asthetic to the museum.
